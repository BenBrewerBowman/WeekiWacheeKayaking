WHAT TO BRING

  • Always bring water for your kayaking or canoe trip on the Weeki Wachee River.
  • Bring snacks or lunch as the trip is usually 2 ½ - 3 hours and you may get hungry.
  • In the summer months bring sunscreen and bug spray.
  • Bring a change of clothes and towels as you may get wet or swim during your trip
  • Bring snorkels and an underwater camera to capture some amazing pictures.
  • We can take care of some of your items; however, you may want to bring a waterproof bag or container for your phone, keys or other valuables.
  • Bring warmer clothes for the winter months

TOURS

Weeki Wachee Kayaking will do everything possible to make your relaxing day kayaking or canoeing the Weeki Wachee River memorable. With crystal clear waters that flows from a magnitude 1 spring, Weeki Wachee is one of the most beautiful rivers in the United States. The majority of the river, with depths ranging from 1 to 12 feet, make it perfect to see the marine life. There is also an abundance of beautiful wildlife on the shore to see as well. We work with you in advance to plan out the tour that fits your needs best.

CALL TO PLAN WITH WEEKI WACHEE KAYAKING

The exceptionally crystal clear water of the Weeki Wachee River allows our guests to see a variety of marine life. Manatee are the main attraction on the river and provide a once in a lifetime for some to see the majestic animals as they traverse the river. Most days provide sightings of manatee cows and calves enjoying the river. Don't miss out on this speical sight and call in to set up a manatee tour.

Weeki Wachee River is home to a diverse marine life. Along with manatees, a variety of fresh water fish live in the river. Turtles and otters can be seen as you travel down the slow winding river with an occasional Florida deer sighting mixed into your trip. Squirrels, rabbits and other animals can be seen almost daily. Several species of birds can be seen including woodpeckers, humming birds, wood cranes, hawks and owls. Don't miss out on the beautiful wildlife of Weeki Wachee and call in to set up a guided tour.

NOTE ABOUT RESERVATIONS

Please call ahead to reserve your boats and trip time, and plan to arrive 15 minutes early to check in. If you happen to run late, let us know as soon as possible so we we can try to work you in for the next soonest departure available.

***Making reservations at least a day in advance gives you the most options for securing the kind of boat you prefer and the departure time you want***

Guests who call for same-day trips may encounter longer telephone hold times and experience delays in river departures due to launches in progress. Please leave a message. Voice mails will be returned as soon as possible to assist you planning your trip.

Walkups are welcome but will be worked in among the reserved, pre-scheduled departures

RULES

  • Alcohol is not permitted on the Weeki Wachee River. Glass bottles are also not permitted.
  • Pets are not permitted unless you launch a private boat from a residence or another public area that allows dogs. Service animals are permitted on the river.
  • You are not allowed to exit your boat in the first mile of the river located inside of the state park.
  • All persons on a kayak or canoe must have a USCG approved flotation device with a whistle. Both are provided to you by Weeki Wachee Kayaking.
  • You are not permitted to touch, feed, molest or ride Manatee. This causes stress, endangers the manatee and can cause pernanent damage or even death.
